What Is a Registered Agent?

A registered agent is the designated contact who receives service of process and official state correspondence on behalf of your company. Every state requires corporations and LLCs to list an agent with a physical in-state address who is available during business hours.

Missing or failing to designate an agent can jeopardize your ability to stay in good standing or respond to legal notices. HNC Registered Agents partners with Registered Agents Inc. so you receive timely notifications, document scans, and annual report filing support through one provider.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a registered agent for an LLC?

Yes. Every state requires LLCs and corporations to maintain a registered agent with a physical address in the state to receive service of process and official notices.

Can I be my own agent?

You can, but you must be available at your listed address during business hours and accept that your address will become part of the public record. Many owners choose a professional service to preserve privacy and avoid missed deliveries.

What does a registered agent do? What is service of process?

A registered agent receives legal documents (service of process) and official mail from the state, then promptly notifies you so you can respond. Registered Agents Inc. scans and uploads documents to your dashboard.

Is an agent required in every state where I do business?

Yes. If you register your company to do business in multiple states, you must appoint a registered agent in each state. HNC Registered Agents connects you with nationwide coverage through one provider.

What’s included for $200/year?

The Registered Agents Inc. service provides a physical in-state address, document acceptance during business hours, digital delivery of service of process and official mail, plus annual report filing included.

How does annual report filing work?

Registered Agents Inc. tracks your due dates, prepares the annual report with the information you provide, and files it on your behalf as part of the service.

What happens if an agent misses a document?

Professional registered agents are staffed during business hours specifically to receive service of process. They promptly notify you so you can respond and avoid default judgments or penalties.

Can I change my registered agent? How?

Yes. After ordering, Registered Agents Inc. provides the forms and instructions to update your registered agent with the state. Most states allow online filings, and the provider can assist with the process.

Are registered agent addresses public?

States publish the registered office address in business registries. Using a professional service keeps your personal address off those public records.

Can non-US founders use this service?

Yes. International founders can appoint Registered Agents Inc. to satisfy state requirements for a domestic registered agent with a physical address.
